## Executive Summary

I have been developing an interactive advocacy dashboard for HAP’s 340B Drug Pricing Program work. The product turns policy data and advocacy messages into a visual tool that hospital CEOs and policymakers can use in meetings and advocacy efforts.

---

## What This Product Is (In Plain Language)

The 340B program lets hospitals and clinics buy outpatient drugs at discounted prices to serve low-income and underserved patients. The dashboard shows:

- **State-by-state status** — Which states have enacted contract pharmacy protection and which have not
- **Pennsylvania’s position** — How the 72 PA hospitals in 340B are affected
- **The advocacy case** — Why protecting 340B matters for patients, hospitals, and communities

It is designed so a CEO or policymaker can understand the landscape, share a link or print a PDF, and use it in advocacy conversations.

## How HAP Data Powers the Dashboard

The dashboard uses HAP’s trusted sources:

- **MultiState** — Legislative status
- **ASHP** — Pharmacy-policy perspective
- **America’s Essential Hospitals** — Advocacy confirmation
- **340B Health / AHA** — Community benefit figures (e.g., $7.95B nationally)
- **HRSA** — Federal oversight and audit context

These feeds are structured so state law status, PA impact, and community benefit metrics can be updated and kept consistent.

## Advocacy Implementation

The dashboard reinforces HAP’s advocacy messages:

1. **Protect 340B and contract pharmacy access** — Reflected in the map and state counts
2. **Patient access** — Stressed in metrics and scenario comparisons
3. **Community benefit** — Highlighted with figures and examples
4. **No cost to taxpayers** — Included in overview and methodology copy

Scenario tools (e.g., expand vs. remove protections) help users see policy impact in simple terms.
