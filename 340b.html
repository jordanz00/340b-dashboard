Overview

What is 340B?

The 340B program requires pharmaceutical manufacturers that participate in Medicaid to sell outpatient drugs at a discounted rate to eligible health care providers. It operates at no cost to taxpayers and helps providers serve vulnerable populations.

HAP Position

Protect 340B integrity and contract pharmacy access.

HAP supports policies that protect the integrity of the 340B program and preserve hospitals' ability to use contract pharmacies to ensure patient access to medications.

HAP asks lawmakers: Protect 340B integrity. Preserve contract pharmacy access for patient medications.

Access to care

Contract pharmacy at risk

Federal guidance allows 340B dispensing via in-house or contract pharmacies. Manufacturers have imposed policies that limit hospitals to one contract pharmacy. Rural and underserved hospitals rely on contract and specialty pharmacies for high-cost specialty drugs.

Pennsylvania safeguards

PA prevents duplicate discounts

  • Manufacturers are not required to provide both 340B discount and Medicaid rebate for the same drug.
  • DHS administers the 340B Drug Exclusion List and invoices manufacturers for Medicaid rebates; covered entities may not dispense 340B-priced drugs on this list.
  • Contract pharmacies dispense non-340B drugs to MA beneficiaries; DHS invoices manufacturers for rebates.
