What you need to know

340B helps vulnerable patients. Drug companies are undermining it.

For over 30 years, 340B has helped hospitals afford discounted medicines for low-income and uninsured patients. It costs taxpayers nothing. Yet some drug makers have illegally stopped giving discounts at community pharmacies — hurting rural and underserved communities.

Simple explainer

What is 340B?

Law requires drug companies that sell to Medicaid to offer discounted prices to hospitals and health centers that serve many uninsured and low-income patients. It's a discount at purchase — not a rebate later. Taxpayers pay nothing.

Impact

What 340B pays for

Free care for uninsured patients. Free vaccines. Mental health. Medication help. Screenings. Dental. Without 340B, communities lose this.

$0B Community benefits from 340B hospitals in 2022 — 47% increase from 2019

The problem

Drug companies are blocking access

Several large drug makers have illegally stopped giving 340B discounts at community and specialty pharmacies. Others want to switch to a rebate model — forcing cash-strapped hospitals to pay full price upfront and wait for refunds. Rural patients rely on these pharmacies for expensive drugs.

Pennsylvania

How PA prevents abuse

PA has strong safeguards: no double discounts, a state exclusion list, and clear rules for contract pharmacies. HAP supports expanding 340B to reach more vulnerable communities — not scaling it back.
