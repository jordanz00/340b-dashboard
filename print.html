Overview

What is 340B?

The 340B Drug Pricing Program allows eligible hospitals and clinics to purchase outpatient drugs at discounted prices to serve low-income and underserved populations. It operates at no cost to taxpayers and helps safety-net providers sustain medication access.

Access to care

Contract pharmacy restrictions hit patient access

Federal guidance permits 340B dispensing through in-house or contract pharmacies. Manufacturer-imposed policies can restrict hospitals to a single contract pharmacy, which puts rural, specialty, and underserved patient access at risk when hospitals depend on distributed pharmacy networks.

Pennsylvania safeguards

PA already prevents duplicate discounts

  • Manufacturers are not required to provide both a 340B discount and a Medicaid rebate for the same drug.
  • DHS administers the 340B Drug Exclusion List and invoices manufacturers for Medicaid rebates, so covered entities may not dispense 340B-priced drugs on this list.
  • Contract pharmacies dispense non-340B drugs to Medicare Advantage beneficiaries, and DHS invoices manufacturers for rebates.
