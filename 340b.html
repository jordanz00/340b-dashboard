Overview

What is 340B?

The 340B program requires pharmaceutical manufacturers participating in Medicaid to sell outpatient drugs at discounted rates to eligible health care providers. It operates at no cost to taxpayers and enables providers to serve vulnerable and underserved populations.

Access to care

Contract pharmacy at risk

Federal guidance permits 340B dispensing through in-house or contract pharmacies. Manufacturer-imposed policies restrict hospitals to a single contract pharmacy. Rural and underserved hospitals depend on contract and specialty pharmacies for high-cost specialty medications.

Pennsylvania safeguards

PA prevents duplicate discounts

  • Manufacturers are not required to provide both 340B discount and Medicaid rebate for the same drug.
  • DHS administers the 340B Drug Exclusion List and invoices manufacturers for Medicaid rebates; covered entities may not dispense 340B-priced drugs on this list.
  • Contract pharmacies dispense non-340B drugs to Medicare Advantage beneficiaries; DHS invoices manufacturers for rebates.
