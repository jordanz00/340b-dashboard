Market Context
7% of U.S. drug market

340B accounts for only 7% of the U.S. drug market, yet manufacturers claim it distorts pricing. The data tells a different story.

What Manufacturers Argue
"340B is growing too fast"
340B accounts for only 7% of total U.S. drug purchases. Growth reflects more hospitals qualifying for the program because they serve low-income and uninsured patients—not abuse.
"Hospitals profit from 340B"
63% of Pennsylvania 340B hospitals operate at a financial loss. 340B savings help offset the cost of uncompensated care and keep safety-net services running.
"Contract pharmacies aren't accountable"
Hospitals face 179 HRSA audits vs only 5 manufacturer audits (FY 2024). 340B entities are subject to extensive federal oversight, while manufacturers restricting access face virtually none.
"Savings don't reach patients"
340B hospitals reported $7.95 billion in community benefit in 2024, funding free prescriptions, cancer screening, behavioral health, and rural clinic operations.

Methodology

Verification order for state law: MultiState, then ASHP, then America's Essential Hospitals. State counts in state-data.js cover 50 states; D.C. appears in data but is excluded from headline totals. All figures trace to named sources above.

Limitations

State law counts change as legislatures meet. Community benefit totals are self-reported aggregates, not independently audited. PDF briefings match HAP March 2026 advocacy materials; confirm against haponline.org if republished.
